Screening tools are brief questionnaires designed to identify whether someone might have a substance use problem. They are not diagnostic tests — they help indicate whether a more thorough evaluation by a healthcare professional is warranted.

## How Screening Works

Substance use screening tools:

- Ask standardized questions about your substance use patterns and their effects
- Take just a few minutes to complete
- Are scored to produce a risk level (low, moderate, high, or severe)
- Have been validated through decades of clinical research
- Are used by healthcare providers worldwide

Screening is a **first step**, not a final answer. A high score doesn't mean you have a diagnosis, and a low score doesn't mean everything is fine if you have concerns.

## Screening Tools Available on Covered

### AUDIT (Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test)
- **Purpose**: Identifies hazardous and harmful alcohol use
- **Questions**: 10
- **Score range**: 0–40
- **Developed by**: World Health Organization (WHO)
- **Score interpretation**:
  - 0–7: Low risk
  - 8–15: Hazardous use (moderate risk)
  - 16–19: Harmful use (high risk)
  - 20–40: Possible dependence (severe risk)

### DAST-10 (Drug Abuse Screening Test)
- **Purpose**: Identifies problematic drug use (excluding alcohol and tobacco)
- **Questions**: 10
- **Score range**: 0–10
- **Developed by**: Harvey Skinner, PhD
- **Score interpretation**:
  - 0: No problems reported
  - 1–2: Low level of problems
  - 3–5: Moderate level of problems
  - 6–8: Substantial level of problems
  - 9–10: Severe level of problems

### CAGE
- **Purpose**: Quick alcohol screening, particularly for identifying dependence
- **Questions**: 4
- **Score range**: 0–4
- **Named for**: Its four questions — **C**ut down, **A**nnoyed, **G**uilty, **E**ye-opener
- **Score interpretation**:
  - 0–1: Low risk
  - 2–4: Clinically significant — further evaluation recommended

## What Your Results Mean

**Low risk** does not necessarily mean no risk. If you have concerns about your substance use, talk to a healthcare provider regardless of your score.

**Moderate or higher risk** means the screening has identified patterns that warrant attention. This could mean:
- Speaking with a healthcare provider for further assessment
- Exploring treatment options
- Making changes to your substance use patterns
- Learning more about substance use disorders

## Privacy and Your Results

On Covered, your screening results are:
- **Computed entirely in your browser** — your answers are never sent to or stored on any server
- **Not saved** unless you choose to save or share them
- **Available in FHIR format** — you can download your results as a FHIR QuestionnaireResponse to share with your healthcare provider or import into an electronic health record

## Important Disclaimers

- Screening tools are **not a diagnosis**. Only a qualified healthcare professional can diagnose a substance use disorder.
- These tools are designed for **adults aged 18 and older**.
- If you are in crisis or immediate danger, call **911**, **988** (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), or **1-800-662-4357** (SAMHSA Helpline).
